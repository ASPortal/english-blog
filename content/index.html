HUMAN HEALTH

Human health is the overall state of physical, mental, and social well-being, not merely the absence of disease or illness.

HEALTH INFLUENCERS

• Genetics
• Lifestyle choices (diet, exercise, sleep)
• Environment (pollution, climate)
• Social connections and relationships
• Access to healthcare • Mental well-being • Economic and educational status
• Public health policies and resources

HEALTH DIMENTIONS

PHYSICAL HEALTH

Physical health refers to the body’s ability to function effectively and efficiently in daily life. It involves maintaining a healthy lifestyle through proper nutrition, regular exercise, adequate sleep, and avoiding harmful behaviors. Good physical health helps prevent chronic diseases, reduces injury risk, and supports overall well-being, enabling the body to perform at its best.

PHYSICAL HEALTH INFLUENCERS

• Nutrition: A balanced intake of essential nutrients, vitamins, and minerals to fuel the body and maintain proper functioning.
• Exercise: Engaging in regular physical activity to improve strength, cardiovascular health, flexibility, and overall fitness.
• Sleep: Sufficient and quality rest for body recovery, mental clarity, and immune system support.
• Hydration: Adequate water intake to support cellular functions, digestion, and overall bodily processes.
• Genetics: Inherited traits that can impact body composition, metabolism, and the risk of certain diseases.
• Mental Health: Positive mental well-being can enhance motivation, reduce stress, and support healthy lifestyle habits.
• Environmental Factors: Exposure to clean air, water, and safe surroundings that reduce the risk of illness and injury.
• Lifestyle Choices: Decisions like avoiding smoking, limiting alcohol intake, and staying away from harmful substances.
• Access to Healthcare: Regular medical checkups and access to preventive and emergency healthcare services.

MENTAL HEALTH

Mental health is a state of cognitive and emotional well-being that impacts how we think, feel, and behave. It involves managing stress, making decisions, forming relationships, and coping with life’s challenges. Good mental health supports resilience, positive self-concept, and the ability to handle daily stressors effectively, contributing to overall well-being and a balanced life.

MENTAL HEALTH INFLUENCERS

• Genetics: Inherited factors that can affect mood, cognition, and susceptibility to mental health conditions.
• Life Experiences: Personal experiences, especially during childhood, such as trauma, abuse, or support systems, which shape mental resilience.
• Social Connections: Quality of relationships with friends, family, and community, offering emotional support and a sense of belonging.
• Lifestyle Choices: Activities like exercise, diet, sleep, and avoiding substance abuse that impact brain function and mood.
• Stress Levels: Chronic stress from work, relationships, or financial issues, which can lead to anxiety, depression, and burnout.
• Physical Health: Physical well-being, as it directly influences energy, motivation, and mental clarity.
• Environment: Exposure to supportive or toxic surroundings, including access to nature and safe spaces.
• Access to Mental Health Resources: Availability of therapy, counseling, and support services for mental health management.

EMOTIONAL HEALTH

Emotional health is the ability to understand, manage, and express emotions in a positive and constructive way. It includes self-awareness, resilience, and the ability to cope with challenges, which all support healthy relationships, decision-making, and overall well-being.

EMOTIONAL HEALTH INFLUENCERS

• Social Connections: The quality and strength of relationships with family, friends, and community that provide support and belonging.
• Life Experiences: Significant events, including trauma, loss, or positive experiences, that shape mental resilience and emotional responses.
• Genetics: Inherited traits that can affect vulnerability to mental health conditions and emotional regulation.
• Lifestyle Choices: Decisions related to exercise, diet, sleep, and substance use that impact mental well-being.
• Stress Levels: Chronic stress from work, relationships, or financial pressures that can contribute to anxiety and depression.
• Physical Health: Overall physical condition, as physical health issues can affect mood and cognitive function.
• Access to Mental Health Resources: Availability of therapy, counseling, and support services that aid in managing mental health.
• Environmental Factors: Conditions in one’s surroundings, including safety, stability, and exposure to toxins, that influence mental well-being.

SOCIAL HEALTH

Social health is the quality of relationships and interactions with others, encompassing the ability to form and maintain positive connections. It involves effective communication, empathy, and the capacity to engage in healthy social networks, contributing to overall well-being and a sense of belonging.

SOCIAL HEALTH INFLUENCERS

• Social Support: Availability of friends, family, and community that provide emotional and practical assistance.
• Communication Skills: Ability to express thoughts and feelings effectively, fostering better relationships.
• Interpersonal Relationships: Quality of relationships with others, including family, friends, and colleagues, which impacts overall social well-being.
• Community Engagement: Participation in social, cultural, and community activities that enhance connection and belonging.
• Cultural Norms: Societal values and norms that influence social behaviors and interactions.
• Life Experiences: Past interactions and experiences that shape social skills and comfort in social situations.
• Mental Health: Emotional well-being that affects how individuals engage and relate to others.
• Environment: The social environment, including safe neighborhoods and supportive communities, that facilitates healthy interactions.
